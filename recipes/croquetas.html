Croquetas Recipe

Image credit to Nano Erdozain on pexels.com

Do you want to impress your Spanish guests with a delicious appetizer? Look no further!

Croqueta is a traditional Spanish appetizer made with shredded meat, coated in breadcrumbs, and deep-fried to golden perfection. What makes it better is that it melts in your mouth.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped choice of meat
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Oil for frying

Now let's get started!

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the flour and cook, stirring constantly, for about 2 minutes until it forms a roux.
  2. Gradually whisk in the milk, ensuring there are no lumps. Continue to cook, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens and comes to a boil.
  3. Remove from heat and stir in the chopped meat. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Let the mixture cool to room temperature.
  4. Once cooled, shape the mixture into small cylindrical or oval-shaped croquetas.
  5. Dip each croqueta into the beaten eggs, then coat with breadcrumbs, ensuring they are fully covered.
  6. Heat oil in a deep fryer or large skillet to 350°F (175°C). Fry the croquetas in batches until they are golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes per batch.
  7. Remove the croquetas from the oil and drain on paper towels. Serve hot with your favorite dipping sauce.
